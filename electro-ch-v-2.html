Electrochemistry Notes

Oxidation vs Reduction

  • Oxidation: Loss of electrons, increase in oxidation state.
    Example: Fe2+ → Fe3+ + e–
  • Reduction: Gain of electrons, decrease in oxidation state.
    Example: Cu2+ + 2e– → Cu

Daniel Cell vs Galvanic Cell

  • Daniel Cell: A specific type of galvanic cell with zinc and copper electrodes in their respective sulfate solutions.
  • Galvanic Cell: Converts chemical energy into electrical energy through spontaneous redox reactions.

Voltaic Cell vs Electrolytic Cell

  • Voltaic Cell: Generates electrical energy from spontaneous chemical reactions.
  • Electrolytic Cell: Consumes electrical energy to drive non-spontaneous chemical reactions.

Electrochemical Cell vs Galvanic Cell

  • Electrochemical Cell: General term for devices that convert between chemical and electrical energy.
  • Galvanic Cell: A type of electrochemical cell where the reaction is spontaneous.

Electrolytic vs Galvanic Cell

  • Electrolytic Cell: Requires external energy input to drive chemical reactions.
  • Galvanic Cell: Produces electrical energy from a chemical reaction.

Electrolysis vs Galvanic Cell

  • Electrolysis: The process of using electricity to decompose compounds.
    Example: Electrolysis of water: 2H2O → 2H2 + O2
  • Galvanic Cell: Uses a redox reaction to produce electricity.

What is Electrolysis?

Definition: Electrolysis is the process of using electrical energy to drive a chemical reaction.

Example: Electrolysis of NaCl: 2NaCl(l) → 2Na(s) + Cl2(g)

Electroplating

Definition: A process that uses electrolysis to deposit a thin layer of metal onto an object.

Example: Silver plating a spoon: Ag+ + e– → Ag

Primary Cell and Secondary Cell

  • Primary Cell: Non-rechargeable, single-use cells (e.g., dry cell).
  • Secondary Cell: Rechargeable cells (e.g., lithium-ion battery).

Construction of a Cell

Parts:

  • Anode and cathode (electrodes)
  • Electrolyte
  • External circuit
