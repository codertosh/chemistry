Electrochemistry Notes

1. Oxidation vs Reduction

  • Oxidation: Loss of electrons, increase in oxidation state, addition of oxygen, or removal of hydrogen.
  • Reduction: Gain of electrons, decrease in oxidation state, removal of oxygen, or addition of hydrogen.
Example:
Oxidation: Zn → Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻
Reduction: Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu

2. Daniel Cell vs Galvanic Cell

  • Daniel Cell: A specific type of galvanic cell with zinc and copper electrodes.
  • Galvanic Cell: A general cell that converts chemical energy into electrical energy via spontaneous redox reactions.
  • Key Difference: Daniel cell is a specific example of a galvanic cell.

3. Voltaic Cell vs Electrolytic Cell

  • Voltaic Cell: Converts chemical energy into electrical energy (spontaneous reaction).
  • Electrolytic Cell: Converts electrical energy into chemical energy (non-spontaneous reaction).
Example:
Voltaic: Daniel Cell
Electrolytic: Electrolysis of water

4. Electrochemical Cell vs Galvanic Cell

  • Electrochemical Cell: General term for any device that converts between chemical and electrical energy.
  • Galvanic Cell: A type of electrochemical cell where energy conversion is spontaneous.

5. Electrolytic vs Galvanic Cell

  • Electrolytic Cell: Requires electrical energy to drive a chemical reaction.
  • Galvanic Cell: Generates electrical energy from a chemical reaction.

6. Electrolysis vs Galvanic Cell

  • Electrolysis: Process of using electricity to cause a chemical reaction (decomposition).
  • Galvanic Cell: Spontaneously produces electricity from a redox reaction.

7. What is Electrolysis?

  • Definition: Breaking a compound into components using electricity.
  • Example: Electrolysis of water: 2H₂O → 2H₂ + O₂

8. Electroplating

  • Definition: Depositing a thin layer of metal onto an object using electrolysis.
  • Example: Galvanization (coating iron with zinc), silver plating jewelry.
  • Setup:
    • Anode: Metal to be deposited.
    • Cathode: Object to be plated.
    • Electrolyte: Solution containing metal ions.

9. Primary Cell vs Secondary Cell

  • Primary Cell: Non-rechargeable, single-use cells (e.g., dry cell, alkaline cell).
  • Secondary Cell: Rechargeable cells (e.g., lead-acid battery, lithium-ion battery).
  • Key Difference: Secondary cells can be recharged multiple times.

10. Construction of a Cell

  • Parts:
    • Electrodes (Anode and Cathode).
    • Electrolyte (solution or paste).
    • Separator (prevents mixing of solutions).
    • External circuit (for electron flow).
